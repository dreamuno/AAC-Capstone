General Questions

Why are there no reports on the home page?

The Academic Assessment Committee has not yet assigned a report to your department. Contact the Academic Assessment Committee if you believe this is an error.

A submitted report was returned to my to-do list with a 'Modify' button. What does this mean?

This means an Academic Assessment Committee member has reviewed your report and returned it to you for modifications. You can see feedback by clicking the 'Feedback' button, and can make the necessary modifications by clicking 'Modify' and navigating to the required section. After the requested modifications have been made, you can resubmit the report when ready.

How do I view reports from previous years?

You can view previous reports by clicking 'Your Reports' in the navigation menu.

How do I view the rubric for the report I am currently working on?

Open the report by clicking 'Start'. On the far-right of the report navigation menu there is the option to 'View Rubric'.

What does the to-do list mean when completing a report?

The to-do list automatically updates as you complete the report. The list in each section covers the materials in that section, as well as prior sections. The to-do list contains two parts: required and suggested. The required section lists what must be completed in the report before you can submit it to the Academic Assessment Committee. If you feel one of these requirements does not apply to your degree program, contact the Academic Assessment Committee, and they will submit the report on your behalf. The suggested section contains automated suggestions based upon the rubric. Sometimes these suggestions will not apply or be accurate for a given degree program. You will be able to submit the report without acting upon any of these suggestions. Please use your best judgement when viewing these suggestions. Finally, the to-do list disappears if there are no more suggestions or requirements to complete.

What is CSV?

The acronym CSV stands for 'comma-separated values'. This file type allows you to export the data within the system in a comma seperated list. CSV files can be easily imported into programs such as Excel, which gives you the option to transfer data for further analysis.

Why aren't all my supplements included in the PDF of the report?

The application can only include files that were already PDF's in the report PDF. Other supplements can still be downloaded seperately.

Administration Questions

How can I see reports to review?

With the top navigation bar, go to the 'Administration' tab. From there, click the 'Reports' link, and you can search for not yet reviewed and submitted reports.

What does 'Generate Reports' do?

When degree programs are created, there is an option to assign a cycle length and starting year. For example, if 2015 is the starting year and 4 is the cycle length, reports should be assigned in 2015, 2019, 2023, 2027, etc. Generating a year's reports assigns a report with the selected rubric to every degree program whose starting year and cycle length combination indicates it should be assigned a report in that given year. However, it does not assign a report if a given degree program has already been manually assigned a report for the given year. Further, not not all degree programs will have a cycle length and starting year, in which case this button will never assign the program a report.

How do I manually assign a report?

From the Departments & Programs page on the navigation bar, find the desired degree program, or department the degree program is in. There will be an option to 'Assign Report', which will take you to the appropriate form.
